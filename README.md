# RIVERS

RIVERS is an open inquiry into how organizations can turn meaningful work into durable, reviewable, and reusable knowledge for people and AI agents.

The repository preserves the protocol's evidence as it evolves. It does not yet define a finished standard, a complete machine-readable schema, or a proven organizational operating system.

## Current posture

The first record, [RIVERS-CORE-0001](./RIVERS/RIVERS-CORE-0001/RIVERS-CORE-0001.md), is the historical conception of RIVERS. It is explicitly non-normative and supersedable.

CORE-0001 presents the initial RIVERS lifecycle:

1. Requirement
2. Implementation
3. Verification
4. Education
5. Retry
6. Standardization

The lifecycle is a conception under investigation, not a mandatory template. Later records may challenge, refine, or supersede it while preserving the evidence for earlier reasoning.

## Evidence and history

Each CORE record should preserve its canonical text alongside the drafts, reviews, responses, discussion summaries, and other evidence that support it. Criticism is evidence: it should inform later records, not be erased from the history of how understanding changed.

The companion material for CORE-0001 is available in its [record package](./RIVERS/RIVERS-CORE-0001/).

## Next inquiry

The planned RIVERS-CORE-0002 will investigate the foundational lifecycle and record boundary for trustworthy organizational knowledge. It will consider, without presuming the outcome, whether the initial RIVERS conception should evolve and whether QUIVERS is justified.
