# Historical and Governance Context

RIVERS-CORE-0001 preserves the original RIVERS lifecycle:

- Requirement
- Implementation
- Verification
- Education
- Retry
- Standardization

CORE-0002 investigates several unresolved issues:

- Invocation threshold
- Requirement versus Question
- The absence of an explicit Understanding stage
- Cheap Question capture versus full record development
- One primary Question as a record boundary
- The full record as a candidate record-level Atomic Unit of Organizational Knowledge

The human founder originally favored transition to QUIVERS and repository renaming.

The first independent review found the direction promising but concluded that the evidence justified only a non-normative investigation, not a normative transition or rename.

The canonical draft therefore uses RIVERS to investigate RIVERS and treats Question and Understanding as candidate improvements rather than adopted protocol stages.

# Required Areas of Review

## 1. Canonical Versus Normative Status

Evaluate whether the draft clearly distinguishes:

- Canonical authority over the historical investigation
- Normative authority over the protocol

Determine whether a reasonable reader could mistakenly conclude that QUIVERS has already replaced RIVERS.

Identify any wording that accidentally converts a conceptual finding into a protocol rule.

## 2. Historical Integrity

Check whether the draft:

- Preserves RIVERS-CORE-0001 as the historical conception
- Avoids backporting Question or Understanding into CORE-0001
- Represents the founder’s stronger transition and naming proposal accurately
- Represents AI qualifications accurately
- Represents the first independent review accurately
- Avoids manufacturing contributor consensus

Use the synthesis note to assess whether interpretive decisions are disclosed honestly.

## 3. Self-hosting Method

Evaluate the decision to retain the RIVERS headings.

Consider whether the draft successfully demonstrates:

- Requirement and Question as distinct functions
- Understanding being awkwardly nested inside Implementation
- RIVERS remaining capable of documenting its own limitations
- Structural strain as qualitative evidence

Determine whether the self-hosting argument is appropriately limited or whether it is being used as stronger evidence than it deserves.

## 4. Question Versus Requirement

Assess whether the draft correctly concludes that Question is conceptually stronger as the general trigger for knowledge investigation.

Check whether it adequately preserves:

- Mandatory requirements
- Regulations
- Contracts
- Work orders
- Operational obligations

Determine whether the draft avoids implying that all mandatory work becomes optional inquiry.

Identify any classes of organizational activity that still do not fit the proposed Question-centered model.

## 5. Understanding

Evaluate whether Understanding is sufficiently:

- Externalized
- Reviewable
- Bounded
- Actionable
- Distinct from Implementation

Review the proposed minimum outputs.

Determine whether Understanding can legitimately:

- Prevent Implementation
- Replace Implementation
- Reframe or split a Question
- Close a Question using existing evidence

Identify whether its current definition risks either vagueness or excessive bureaucracy.

## 6. Invocation Threshold

Evaluate the three-threshold model:

1. Capture threshold
2. Development threshold
3. Authority threshold

Determine whether:

- The distinction is conceptually sound
- CORE-0002 appropriately focuses on the first two
- The authority threshold is a justified canonical-author inference
- The invocation problem has been described as improved and relocated rather than solved

Assess whether broad, low-friction capture is stated carefully enough to avoid implying permanent unbounded retention.

## 7. Captured Question Identity

Evaluate the provisional description of a captured Question as a lightweight precursor state.

Determine whether the draft successfully avoids creating:

- A parallel knowledge container
- A second AUK-like object
- An unexplained duplicate artifact

Assess whether the requirement for traceable continuity is strong enough while appropriately leaving the implementation model unresolved.

## 8. Record Boundary and AUK

Evaluate:

> One full record should answer one primary Question.

Determine whether this provides a coherent boundary for:

- Evidence
- Review
- Responsibility
- Publication
- Citation
- Supersession

Then evaluate:

> A complete evidence-bearing primary-Question record is the leading candidate for the record-level Atomic Unit of Organizational Knowledge.

Assess whether the distinction between record-level and claim-level atomicity is sufficient.

Identify whether “Atomic Unit of Organizational Knowledge” remains a useful term or whether the qualifications make it misleading.

## 9. Lifecycle Flexibility

Assess whether the principle:

> Lifecycle stages are matters that must be considered and dispositioned, not mandatory volumes of work or prose.

provides enough protection against write amplification and empty ritual.

Determine whether the draft sufficiently clarifies:

- Skippable Implementation
- Verification without Implementation
- Retry with no further action
- Standardization with no standard

Identify any risk that “conditional execution” makes the lifecycle too vague.
