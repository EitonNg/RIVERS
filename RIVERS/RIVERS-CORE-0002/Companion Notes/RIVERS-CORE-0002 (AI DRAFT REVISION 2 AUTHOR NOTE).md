## Major Changes Made

* Retained the RIVERS lifecycle headings and added a methodological notice explaining why RIVERS is being used to investigate its own possible revision.
* Separated the need for the investigation from the primary Question inside Requirement.
* Added Pre-implementation Understanding inside Implementation and treated the awkward placement as self-hosting evidence.
* Preserved requirements, mandates, regulations, tickets, and contractual obligations as inputs and constraints rather than implying that Question makes them disappear.
* Recast universal Question capture as broad, low-friction capture subject to provenance, access, spam, retention, and privacy constraints.
* Clarified that the two-tier model relocates the threshold problem into triage rather than eliminating it.
* Treated a captured Question as an initial lifecycle state or backlog event, not a second knowledge container.
* Qualified AUK as a record-level role or maturity property of a complete primary-Question record while separating claim-level atomicity.
* Added conditional lifecycle semantics to avoid mandatory empty sections.
* Expanded Verification into conceptual, self-hosting, peer-review, limitation, test-case, and findings-classification analyses.
* Distinguished lifecycle state, Question outcome, and publication or governance status.
* Replaced immediate naming proposals with deferred investigation.

## Claims Strengthened

The revision gives stronger, more explicit support to these claims:

* Question is more general than Requirement as a trigger for knowledge investigation.
* Requirements remain necessary as mandates, constraints, and organizational inputs.
* Understanding has a distinct, reviewable function that is not represented clearly in the current lifecycle.
* Cheap capture and expensive trust formation are different invocation decisions.
* One primary Question provides a coherent candidate record boundary.
* The complete evidence-bearing record is the leading candidate for a record-level AUK.

These claims are strengthened as conceptual findings, not as normative protocol rules.

## Claims Weakened or Qualified

* “Accept all Questions” was qualified to broad, low-friction capture of organizationally relevant knowledge gaps subject to unresolved controls.
* “The invocation threshold is solved” was replaced with the finding that the threshold is improved and relocated into triage.
* “A full record is the AUK” was qualified to the leading candidate for record-level atomicity; claim-level atomicity remains unresolved.
* “QUIVERS is better than RIVERS” was replaced with conceptual advantages in the candidate Question-and-Understanding model.
* LLM-supported clustering and triage were classified as plausible but untested.
* Immediate lifecycle and repository renaming proposals were classified as not currently justified.

## Disagreement With the Peer Reviewer

There is no material disagreement with the reviewer's evidentiary conclusions.

The revision adds one nuance: broad capture remains a meaningful design objective even though literal unbounded permanent storage is not justified. This is consistent with the reviewer's distinction between low-friction authenticated capture and an unmanaged question pool.

## Evidence or Repository Files Not Located

All files named as primary evidence in the revision task were located. Relevant CORE-0001 canonical and companion review materials were also located.

No pilot reports, production question-pool evidence, comparative RIVERS/QUIVERS trials, or empirical LLM-triage results were present. The revised draft therefore treats all operational performance claims as untested.
