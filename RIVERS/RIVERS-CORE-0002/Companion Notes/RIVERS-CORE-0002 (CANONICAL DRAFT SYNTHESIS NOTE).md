## 1. Source Hierarchy Used

1. The canonical RIVERS-CORE-0001 record establishes the historical RIVERS lifecycle and the unresolved questions inherited by CORE-0002.
2. The original CORE-0002 human draft is the primary source for founder intent, including the proposed Question-and-Understanding lifecycle, broad Question capture, full-record AUK, and naming proposal.
3. The formal-English human edit is editorial evidence of the same intent, not an independent proposal.
4. The initial AI draft is the primary technical synthesis of the human proposal and its evidentiary limitations.
5. The peer-review prompt establishes the adversarial review standard; the independent peer review is the principal critical assessment.
6. The AI revision prompt, AI Draft Revision 2, and its author note document how the review was integrated.
7. The canonical-draft prompt governs this synthesis but is not substantive evidence that the investigated claims are true.
8. Relevant CORE-0001 drafts, reviews, and conversation evidence provide historical grounding but do not override the canonical CORE-0001 record.

## 2. Major Synthesis Decisions

* Used the existing RIVERS lifecycle rather than structuring the record as QUIVERS.
* Marked the record as a canonical non-normative investigation with publication approval pending.
* Distinguished canonical authority over the investigation's conclusions from normative protocol authority.
* Made Requirement and Question perform separate functions.
* Nested pre-implementation Understanding inside Implementation and treated the awkwardness as qualitative self-hosting evidence.
* Adopted three conceptual thresholds: capture, development, and authority.
* Used cautious “lightweight precursor state” terminology for a captured Question.
* Preserved identity continuity as a requirement while leaving the promotion mechanism unresolved.
* Qualified AUK explicitly as record-level rather than claim-level.
* Used Education and Standardization in the candidate lifecycle for continuity with current RIVERS while stating that final grammatical naming is unresolved.

## 3. Human Proposals Retained

* Question as the general trigger for knowledge investigation.
* Understanding as a gate and shaper of Implementation.
* The possibility that Understanding can answer a Question without Implementation.
* Low-friction capture of Questions whose later value may not yet be visible.
* One primary Question per full record.
* The full record, rather than a separate artifact, as the candidate AUK.
* QUIVERS and repository renaming as historical proposals, preserved in source distinctions and companion evidence but not adopted.

## 4. AI Contributions Retained

* The two-tier separation between Question capture and full development.
* Explicit triage outcomes and evidentiary limits.
* The distinction between a captured Question and a full record.
* Question-outcome classifications.
* Separation of lifecycle state, Question outcome, and publication status.
* Conditional lifecycle execution to avoid empty ritual sections.
* Classification of findings by evidentiary strength.
* The list of required pilots and future investigations.

## 5. Reviewer Criticisms Incorporated

* The threshold is relocated into triage rather than eliminated.
* Literal unbounded capture is not justified.
* Question-pool governance, privacy, retention, provenance, and authority remain unresolved.
* A captured Question must not recreate the rejected dual-container model.
* A record may be atomic for governance while containing multiple reusable claims.
* LLM triage remains plausible but untested and cannot receive automatic authority.
* Self-hosting is qualitative evidence, not empirical validation.
* Normative transition and public renaming are not justified.

## 6. Claims Deliberately Weakened

* “Accept all Questions” became broad, low-friction capture subject to controls and retention decisions.
* “The invocation threshold is solved” became “improved and relocated into triage.”
* “The full record is the AUK” became “the leading candidate for the record-level AUK.”
* “QUIVERS is better than RIVERS” became “the candidate model has conceptual advantages.”
* AI/LLM value in triage was classified as plausible but untested.
* The protocol and repository rename moved from intended action to unsupported proposal.

## 7. Claims Deliberately Excluded

* Empirical superiority of QUIVERS.
* Normative adoption or supersession of RIVERS.
* Approval of any lifecycle, protocol, or repository rename.
* Universal permanent capture of every Question.
* A complete production question-pool, permission, retention, or promotion model.
* LLM decision authority.
* A final AUK ontology, claim model, or normative state machine.

## 8. Remaining Unresolved Questions

* How captured Questions retain identity and provenance through promotion or archival.
* Who has authority to triage, merge, split, archive, promote, approve, or standardize.
* How privacy, access control, retention, deletion, and malicious content are handled.
* How AI reliability, majority bias, and minority risk signals are evaluated.
* How epistemic states, evidence quality, and confidence are represented.
* How conflict, supersession, and deprecation operate.
* Whether claim-level identifiers and citation are needed inside record-level AUKs.
* Whether the model works across domains and at organizational scale.
* What evidence threshold would justify superseding RIVERS.

* **Naming:** The founder's transition and rename proposal is historically material, but evidentiary restraint governs the canonical conclusion.
* **Candidate-stage grammar:** Education and Standardization are used for continuity; the final terminology remains unresolved.
