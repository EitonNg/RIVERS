# Follow-up Peer Review Request — RIVERS-CORE-0001

Thank you for your detailed and constructive review of the conception of the RIVERS protocol.

Your review challenged several foundational assumptions, particularly regarding the object model, epistemic states, governance, conflict resolution, and the distinction between documentation and knowledge evolution. Rather than treating the review as criticism to rebut, we used it as evidence to continue the protocol's own evolution.

Since receiving your review, we have conducted an extensive series of discussions examining your observations from first principles. These discussions have resulted in several significant conceptual changes, including—but not limited to—the following:

* Reframing the protocol around **Questions** rather than **Requirements**, based on the observation that organizational knowledge is created by answering questions. Requirements, regulations, customer requests, defects, and opportunities all become questions that can be investigated.
* Introducing **Understanding** as a distinct stage whose purpose is to establish a shared, evidence-based understanding of the problem before implementation begins. This stage captures definitions, assumptions, constraints, scope, success criteria, and unknowns.
* Realizing that the protocol record itself is the **Atomic Unit of Organizational Knowledge**, rather than maintaining a separate AUK object. The concept of an Atomic Unit of Knowledge remains, but it is now considered a property of the protocol record rather than a separate architectural type.
* Strengthening the principle that **one protocol record should answer one primary question**, providing a natural boundary for organizational knowledge.
* Clarifying that the protocol's purpose is not to create documentation, but to capture the complete lifecycle through which knowledge becomes trustworthy and reusable.

These discussions have also raised the possibility that the protocol may ultimately evolve from **RIVERS** to **QUIVERS**. However, we have deliberately not treated that as a foregone conclusion. Instead, we intend to investigate that architectural change through a subsequent CORE record so that the protocol evolves through evidence rather than retrospective editing.

Accordingly, we have **not** rewritten the conception of CORE-0001 to incorporate later discoveries. We intend for CORE-0001 to represent the best understanding available at the time of the protocol's conception, preserving its historical reasoning. Later conceptual advances will be captured through additional CORE records.

## Request

We are not asking you to rewrite the document.

Instead, we ask you to perform a second independent review.

Specifically:

1. Have the conceptual weaknesses identified in your original review been adequately addressed through the subsequent discussions?
2. Are there any major unresolved architectural concerns that would prevent publication of **RIVERS-CORE-0001** as the historical conception of the protocol?
3. Do you believe the protocol has become conceptually stronger through this review cycle?
4. Would you recommend publishing **RIVERS-CORE-0001** as the first historical record, with subsequent conceptual evolution captured through additional CORE records rather than rewriting history?

If you believe CORE-0001 is still not ready for publication, please identify only the remaining foundational issues that must be resolved before publication.

If you believe it is ready, please explicitly state that recommendation and explain why.

Our objective is not to achieve unanimous agreement, but to ensure that the protocol's first published record meets a standard of conceptual rigor that future contributors can build upon with confidence.
