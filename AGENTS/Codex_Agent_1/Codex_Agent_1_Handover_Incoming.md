# RIVERS Repository — Founding Agent Handoff

You are entering the public RIVERS repository at an important historical milestone.

RIVERS was conceived through an extended collaboration between a human founder, an AI co-author, and independent AI reviewers. The repository now contains the materials associated with `RIVERS-CORE-0001`, the first historical conception record of the protocol.

Your first responsibility is **understanding and stewardship**, not immediate implementation.

## Repository Context

RIVERS began as a proposed lifecycle for converting meaningful organizational work into durable knowledge:

* Requirement
* Implementation
* Verification
* Education
* Retry
* Standardization

`RIVERS-CORE-0001` preserves that original conception.

After CORE-0001 was drafted and independently reviewed, later discussions produced important new ideas, including:

* Question as the generative unit of knowledge
* Understanding as a distinct problem-framing and implementation-gating stage
* One primary question per record
* The record itself as the Atomic Unit of Organizational Knowledge
* The possible evolution of RIVERS into QUIVERS:

  * Question
  * Understanding
  * Implementation
  * Verification
  * Educate
  * Retry
  * Standardize

These later discoveries must **not** be silently inserted into or used to rewrite `RIVERS-CORE-0001`.

They belong in subsequent CORE records, beginning with the planned `RIVERS-CORE-0002`, which will investigate the foundational lifecycle and possible transition to QUIVERS.

The conclusion of CORE-0002 must not be assumed merely because the current direction appears positive.

## Founding Principles

Preserve these principles unless a later, properly evidenced CORE record challenges them:

1. **History is evidence.**
   Earlier reasoning must not be silently rewritten to make the protocol appear more mature or internally consistent than it was at the time.

2. **One record should address one primary question or cohesive matter.**
   Do not create documents merely to distribute related prose across more files.

3. **Evidence belongs inside the record it supports.**
   Drafts, reviews, objections, responses, measurements, and discussion summaries are intrinsic parts of the record’s evidentiary chain.

4. **Do not create a parallel history system.**
   A CORE record must be capable of preserving its own provenance and evidence.

5. **Criticism is evidence.**
   Do not optimize for agreement with the founder, prior AI agents, or existing documents.

6. **Distinguish historical description from normative specification.**
   CORE-0001 is a historical, non-normative, supersedable conception record.

7. **Do not invent decisions.**
   Clearly distinguish:

   * Existing repository facts
   * Previously accepted decisions
   * Your interpretations
   * Open questions
   * New proposals

8. **Human and AI contributions are complementary.**
   AI may analyze, challenge, draft, review, and modify repository artifacts, but it must not conceal AI authorship or represent an unapproved proposal as organizational consensus.

9. **Standardization remains challengeable.**
   A current default is not permanent truth.

10. **The protocol is independent of its tools.**
    GitHub, Codex, Markdown, schemas, databases, knowledge graphs, and future applications are implementations or interfaces—not RIVERS itself.

11. **Avoid tooling sprawl.**
    Do not begin building a CLI, web application, plugin, graph database, or formal schema until the relevant conceptual questions and requirements have been investigated.

12. **Prefer conceptual honesty over apparent completeness.**
    Unknown, unresolved, contested, and unverified matters should remain visibly so.
